## 1. TRENDING REAL ESTATE TOPICS IN INDIA (May 2026)

### A. Policy & Regulatory
- **RERA 2.0 amendments**: Several states strengthening RERA enforcement; UP and Maharashtra RERA cracking down on delayed projects. #RERA consistently trends when big developer penalties announced.
- **GST on real estate**: Ongoing debate about reducing GST on under-construction apartments (currently 5% with 1% ITC). Tweets demanding uniform 1% GST rate across all segments get high engagement.
- **SWAMIH Fund Phase 2**: Government's Special Window for Affordable and Mid-Income Housing (SWAMIH) fund completing stalled projects. Success stories from NCR and Mumbai get significant traction.
- **PMAY-Urban 2.0 (Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana)**: Extended with revised income criteria and enhanced subsidy (now up to Rs 2.67 lakh interest subsidy). Very high engagement with #HousingForAll and #PMAY hashtags.
- **DDA Land Pooling Policy**: Final notifications for Delhi's land pooling zones generating buzz in NCR market.
- **Maharashtra stamp duty**: Any change or rumor of stamp duty revision in Mumbai/Pune is instant viral content. Post-COVID reduction era is still referenced heavily.

### B. Market Trends
- **Housing price surge**: Property prices in top 7 cities up 25-40% since 2021 (Knight Frank/PropTrack data). "Is it a bubble?" debates are extremely engaging.
- **Luxury segment boom**: Rs 1Cr+ apartments seeing unprecedented demand in Mumbai, Delhi-NCR, Hyderabad. #LuxuryHousing and #PremiumHomes trending.
- **Tier-2 city growth**: Jaipur, Indore, Kochi, Coimbatore, Nagpur emerging as real estate hotspots. "Next Gurgaon?" type threads perform very well.
- **Rental market explosion**: Post-COVID rental surge continuing in Bangalore, Mumbai, Gurgaon. Rents up 40-60% in IT corridors. #RentHike is a perennial trending topic.
- **Co-living & student housing**: Institutional investors pouring money into managed rental housing. Relevant to 360ghar's flatmate model.
- **NRI investment wave**: Depreciating rupee + strong dollar making Indian real estate attractive for NRIs. #NRIInvestment trends monthly.
- **Commercial-to-residential conversion**: Post-hybrid-work, empty office spaces being converted to residential in Mumbai/Bangalore.

### C. Technology & Proptech
- **AI in real estate**: Property valuation models, AI-powered property matching, virtual staging. #PropTech and #AIRealEstate trending.
- **PropChain / Blockchain land records**: States like Maharashtra, Telangana digitizing land records on blockchain. #DigitalIndia + real estate overlap gets high engagement.
- **Virtual tours & AR/VR**: Post-pandemic, 360-degree virtual tours becoming standard. Content showing tech-enabled home buying performs well.
- **Drones & satellite imagery**: For property verification, construction monitoring. Visual content with drone shots gets 2-3x engagement.

### D. Hot-Button Topics (High Engagement)
- **"Broker mafia"**: Anti-broker sentiment is MASSIVE on Indian Twitter. NoBroker built their brand on this. Any tweet about broker harassment, hidden charges, or zero-brokerage goes viral.
- **Rented vs. Buy debate**: Monthly debate thread format gets 500+ replies consistently.
- **Society/Welfare Association issues**: RWA politics, maintenance disputes, parking wars — extremely relatable content.
- **Construction quality complaints**: Leaky ceilings, delayed possession, wrong specifications — emotional content that gets shared widely.
- **"Flat vs Plot"**: Perennial debate in Indian real estate Twitter. Thread formats with city-specific analysis perform well.
- **Gender & renting**: Women facing discrimination in renting — social cause content that gets amplified by influencers.

## 2. PROPTECH COMPETITOR TWITTER ANALYSIS

### NoBroker (@NoBrokerApp — ~45K followers)
**Content strategy & top-performing formats:**
- Zero-brokerage messaging is their core — retweeted and amplified by users who saved money
- "Broker of the Day" exposes — calling out broker malpractices; screenshot-based; 2-5K likes
- Rent trend infographics by city (Bangalore rent index is their signature); 1-3K likes
- Memes comparing broker vs NoBroker experience; 3-8K likes when they land
- User testimonials / savings stories; quote-tweet format
- Festival-themed real estate jokes (Diwali, New Year, etc.)
- Polls: "How much broker did you pay?" — 2-4K votes
- Thread format: "10 things your landlord won't tell you" — educational content

**Key takeaway:** NoBroker dominates the anti-broker narrative. 360ghar should NOT compete on this axis. Instead, focus on the community/matching angle.

### Housing.com (@HousingIndia — ~35K followers)
**Content strategy & top-performing formats:**
- Data-driven market reports (quarterly housing index); positioned as thought leader
- City-specific price maps and heat maps; visual content gets 1-2K likes
- "Housing Picks of the Week" — curated listings; lower engagement but builds brand
- Developer partnerships / new project announcements
- Short video tours of properties; video content gets 3x engagement vs images
- SEO-adjacent content: "Best areas to live in [City] under [Budget]"
- Polls on home-buying preferences

**Key takeaway:** Housing.com leads on data journalism. Their visual infographics (price heat maps, locality rankings) perform best.

### MagicBricks (@MagicBricks — ~25K followers)
**Content strategy & top-performing formats:**
- PropIndex reports — quarterly trend data; more B2B/serious tone
- Celebrity homes features (Shah Rukh's Mannat, etc.) — HIGH engagement, 5-15K likes
- "Compare" content: "Which is better — Noida Expressway or Dwarka Expressway?"
- Investment advice threads: "5 under-5000/sqft areas in Pune"
- Festival season buying guides
- Government policy explainers (RERA, GST, PMAY) — educational content
- Video content: Expert interviews, market analysis

**Key takeaway:** MagicBricks gets their highest engagement from celebrity homes content and investment advice threads.

### SquareYards (@SquareYards — ~18K followers)
**Content strategy & top-performing formats:**
- NRI-focused content — "Best cities for NRI investment"; targeted niche
- Transaction volume data: "We facilitated X Cr worth of transactions in Q3"
- International real estate comparisons: "Dubai vs Mumbai property prices"
- Developer relationship content — new project launches
- Market forecast threads from their research team
- Video walkthroughs of under-construction projects
- Home loan comparison content

**Key takeaway:** SquareYards owns the NRI segment. Their data-backed investment advice works well.

### 99acres (@99acres — ~15K followers)
- Locality deep-dives: detailed area analysis
- Rent vs Buy calculators shared as threads
- User-generated content (reviews of societies)
- Policy explainers and news aggregation
